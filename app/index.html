    Not Just a Trilogy

    Phase 1: Iowa Roots | 1980-2001

    I was born and raised in Iowa. I completed a two-year degree in LAN Management at Kirkwood Community College. During this time, a trip starting in Cancun, Mexico and then sidetracking to Belize and Guatemala with a friend, changed my life forever. This experience sparked a larger desire to see the world. I went on more trips including a multi-country excursion from Mexico all the way to Panama and Peru. I also took a trip to Hawaii, which motivated me to move there and continue my education.

    Phase 2: Paradise Found | 2002-2007

    Before graduating and moving to Hawaii I began studying Japanese. I decided to pursue a degree in Asian Studies in a place like Oahu because of the large prevalence of Japanese culture. During the beginning of my studies I was awarded the Honda Scholarship to study in Osaka, Japan for a year. The time in Japan was by far the most rewarding thing in my life because I was able to fully immerse myself in Japanese language and culture. Japanese culture really clicked with me and I soaked up the zen nature of the society. Upon completing my study abroad, I returned to Hawaii to finish my degree. At that time, an old friend invited me to teach a technology course at a school that he worked at in Bangkok, Thailand.

    Phase 3: Land of Smiles | 2008-2013 & 2014

    I moved to Thailand and all in all spent about 6 years teaching English to students ranging from kindergarten to middle school. Being involved in the educational system proved to be very rewarding for me. During this time I consistently tried to enrich myself with studying the Thai language and being immersed in the international melting pot known as Bangkok. I really enjoyed living in Thailand but I was far from family and I didn’t see a lot of opportunity to build a career. I decided to return to the U.S. and join my family who had migrated to Atlanta.

    Current Phase: City of Trees | 2013, 2015 and Present

    I immediately fell in love with Atlanta’s lush green trees coupled with its creative energy and thriving technological scene. While I knew Atlanta was the perfect fit for me, I was still struggling to find the right career path. Drawing on my previous experience in LAN, I started to study for a Cisco Certification until I heard about the Iron Yard. I spent a little over a month prepping for this intensive course which proved to be the most challenging thing I have ever done. The course pushed me to my limit, and I came out stronger because it. During this time I have developed a love for JavaScript and its many related technologies. Being able to build and create things is a truly self-gratifying experience. Now that I have found my passion, I am dedicated on building a long and successful career in this city of trees I now call home.

    Work

    Skinny Ties

    As a developer we are often tasked with creating a website from an image provided to us from a designer. During the first week of school at The Iron Yard, we were assigned this task using HTML, CSS, Sip, and the Perfect Pixel tool. Getting things to line up just right was surprisingly challenging and time consuming, and it gave me a new appreciation for web design.

    Github | Live Site

    Simple Calculator

    During the second week we began to learn JavaScript, and in particular for this assignment we were learning the basics of working with the DOM. This simple calculator can add, subtract, multiply, and divide whole numbers. Figuring out the logic in making a calculator work was exhilarating and was my first step into real programming.

    Github | Live Site

    Etsy Live

    In week three we continued to work on creating a website based on an image; however in this case we also used jQuery for DOM manipulation. We used Ajax with JSON objects to pull in live data from the Etsy API to populate our site. When the live data finally came in, I was able to dig deep into the JSON data and pull out what I needed. It was a great feeling of accomplishment.

    Github | Live Site

    Duck Face

    Our first major group project was in week four. We were given a write-up from a client detailing what they were looking for in the creation of their website. In addition we learned all the technical skills such as collaborating on Git and dealing with Flickr's Photo API. The project required a great deal of teamwork and learning how to divide up modular pieces of work so that everyone could contribute.

    Github | Live Site

    Around The World

    Moving on to week six, we built a couple of simple contact lists using Backbone and React. In this project we were tasked with creating an Instagram-like clone. We used Backbone to make Models, Collections, and Routes for the application. We used React to write all of the components. In this application users can add and edit images stored in the Parse database. This was one of the more taxing times for me in the course because we went from using a few files for making a website to using many files to build a modular application.

    Github | Live Site

    Smash Card

    At the end of week seven we were assigned our second group project. This was our first project where we collaborated with Back-end students to set up a login and perform the CRUD operations. Our task was to create a flashcard game to help users study. Users can add decks and cards based on any topics that they select. In this project we used Backbone and React to set up different Routes for our application. Learning how to communicate with the Back-end students and furthering my knowledge of Backbone and React gave me a more holistic view of web programming.

    Github | Live Site

    Anime is fun!

    In week eight we built our first major Angular application requiring us to handle all of the CRUD procedures from start to finish. We used controllers, services, routers, and many of Angular's built in directives to do this. We also worked with the Parse database to store changes to the images and data on the site. After gaining experience working with larger applications in Backbone and React, learning Angular seemed more manageable for me. I started to see how frameworks function.

    Github | Live Site

    Bill Splitter

    This was a project that we worked on in which we used Angular to connect to the server and database created by the Back-end students on our team. This was my first ever Hackathon. It started Friday evening at the end of week eight and continued all the way to Sunday afternoon. At that time we gave our sales pitch for our project to our instructors and volunteers from the Codesmith team. While overcoming a short time limit and very little sleep, we managed to put together a MVP application to help people who have roommates split the rent and utility bills with as little effort as possible.

    Github | Live Site

    Five Words

    This was our final group project. During this project I worked with another Front-end Engineer and a Rails Developer, to put together an application for meeting friends based on the matching of simple words. We decided to use Angular for this project. Although I had used it in the past, it was amazing to learn Angular not only through lectures, but also by simply discovering something that we wanted our app to do and finding a way to make it possible. This was done through Angular or sometimes just writing some basic Vanilla JavaScript. Presenting this project at Demo Day and seeing people truly interested in what you made was something I will never forget. With this project I also built a new level of confidence in my ability to teach myself any of the necessary skills to pursue this career.
